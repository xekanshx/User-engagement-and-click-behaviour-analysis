Model Training - KNN Collaborative Filtering
We train a K-Nearest Neighbours model to find users with similar sport preferences.

Key design decisions:

Model is trained only on 831 users with actual click history not all 8,813 users
Sport profile vectors are L1-normalised so users with more visits don't dominate
K=10 neighbours balances personalisation with sufficient article coverage
Why KNN for recommendation: KNN finds users who consume similar sport content and borrows their click behaviour.

## Recommendation Engine — Segment-Aware KNN

The recommendation function personalises results based on two signals:
**who the user is similar to** and **how exploratory their segment is**.

**Segment-based sport diversity rule:**
- Casual users → recommend from top 1 sport only (84% sport loyal in our analysis)
- Regular users → recommend from top 2 sports
- Power users → recommend from top 3 sports (most exploratory at 65.6% sport loyal)

**Recommendation logic:**
1. Find 10 nearest neighbours by sport profile similarity
2. Collect articles those neighbours clicked
3. Filter by allowed sports for this user's segment
4. Weight each article by the neighbour's CAS score — higher affinity neighbours get more trust
5. Rank by weighted score and return top 5

**Cold start handling:**
Users outside the 831 with click history receive popular articles within their top sport — ensures every user gets a recommendation regardless of history.

**Evaluation metrics:**
- Sport match rate — do recommendations match sports the user actually engages with? (primary metric)
- Exact precision — do recommendations exactly match articles the user clicked? (secondary metric)

**Results:**
- Sport match rate: 65.6%
- Exact precision: 1.2% (expected low — 1,544 candidate articles, only 5 recommended)
